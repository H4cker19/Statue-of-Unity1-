About

Saradar Vallabhbhai Patel

Former Deputy Prime minister of india

Sardar Vallabhbhai Patel was a leader of India’s freedom movement. He was born on October 31, 1875, in a small village in Gujarat. The word ‘Sardar’ means a chief leader. This title was bestowed upon him by Mahatma Gandhi during the freedom struggle. As a freedom fighter who did not cooperate with the British, he was jailed many times during the struggle for independence. After India was free from British rule, Sardar Patel worked hard to bring the entire country together for a strong national integration. This is why he is called the ‘Iron Man of India’. Soon after India got independence, he was elected as the Home Minister and later Deputy Prime Minister. He was the recipient of the Bharat Ratna Award in 1991. As a national leader, he firmly believed in the power of unity. It was this belief that helped him bring divided India together. His birth anniversary is observed as ‘Rashtriya Ekta Divas’ or the ‘National Unity Day in India so that we never forget his message of strength in being united. Sardar Patel’s statue in Gujarat is known as the Statue of Unity. It is a popular destination, being the tallest statue in the world.
